Guten Tag! Zdravo! Ciao!

I have visited Europe twice, and seen 3 countries: Italy, Croatia, and Germany! I truly enjoyed all 3 countries; it is hard to pick a favorite. They are all special for different reasons. I visited Italy in Winter 2008 and Germany/Croatia in Summer 2012.

Berlin, Germany

In Summer 2012, I traveled to Europe for the second time to visit my friend Ramina who was living in Berlin at the time. Germany is such a cool country and Berlin was a very exciting city. We visited plenty of World War II memorials, including Hitler's bunker! Seeing the Berlin Wall was a real treat. Overall, I was very impressed with the German people, particularly their discipline, hard-working nature, and good-heartedness.

Germany's Biergartens are not to be missed! There, I enjoyed many ice cold Berliners among the tranquil scenery.




Dubrovnik, Croatia

While visiting Ramina in Berlin, we took a 5-day trip to Dubrovnik, Croatia, a city that Ramina had always wanted to visit, so I agreed to tag along. And boy am I glad that I did. What a gorgeous city; easily one of my favorites in the whole world! We rented a little apartment against the hillside, and took a 30 minute walk downtown (downhill) and back (uphill) everyday. We didn't mind because it was brutally hot that July, and we needed 2 double scoop gelatos per day in order to stay cool. Come on...it was a matter of survival!

The highlight of Croatia was cliff diving on the beautiful island of Lokrum. Here is a video showing just how crazy we were!



10 Cities in 10 Days: An Italian Voyage

During my junior year of college, my 2 close high school friends and I visited Italy through a college-student organized tour. The itinerary said we'd visit 10 cities in 10 days, and that we did. In chronological order, we saw: Milan, Verona, Venice, Florence, Sienna, Assisi, Pompei, Sorrento, the Vatican, and Rome! What a nutty schedule it was, but it was so awesome to see the entire country in such a short and convenient way. My family roots are from Italy, so this was a very special trip for me. We spent New Year's in Florence near the Michaelangelo statue, carrying a bottle of champagne in the streets along with thousands of others. It was awesome! I wasn't even 21 yet! But it was perfectly legal in Italy.

Here is one of my favorite memories in Rome: throwing a penny into the ancient Trevi fountain.
